A Quantum Cryptosystem Secure From Third-Party Interference

Tanishka Mehta | The Academy of Science | US

Billions of people worldwide use cryptography to securely transmit information. As technology is becoming faster and more powerful, computing power is increasing. Eavesdroppers can hack classical encryption keys within minutes, compromising the safety of information and risking millions of lives. Therefore, researchers are turning to quantum encryption since it is harder to hack due to quantum advantage. Although quantum cryptography protocols currently exist, they have limitations that greatly decrease protocol efficiency. Research will be conducted on randomly polarizing qubit sequences and sending them to the receiver efficiently without third-party interference. The algorithm will also determine whether third-parties exist and the amount of information they know.
